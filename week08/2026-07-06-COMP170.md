## Revisiting the Quadratic Equation
We picked back up with the quadratic equation $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$ to introduce how we write methods. We reviewed the discriminant $\delta = b^2 - 4ac$ and the solution formula $x_{1,2} = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{\delta}}{2a}$, then talked about what happens when $\delta$ is negative: the equation still has solutions, just not real ones.

## Imaginary and Complex Numbers
We introduced the imaginary unit $i$, defined so that $i^2 = -1$, which lets us take the square root of a negative number. A complex number like $z = x + yi$ has a real part and an imaginary part, and we noted that real numbers are just complex numbers with an imaginary part of zero.

## Tuples vs. Lists
We compared tuples to lists we already know. Lists use square brackets and don't care about order — `["Chicago", "Illinois"]` is the same as `["Illinois", "Chicago"]`. Tuples use parentheses, and order matters, which makes them the right container for an ordered pair of solutions.

## Designing a Method to Solve the Equation
With Bella's and Andrew's help walking through examples, we worked out the special cases our method needs to handle before computing anything: if $a = 0$ the equation isn't quadratic anymore (it becomes linear, or if $b = 0$ too, it's not a valid equation at all unless $c = 0$). We planned to represent each solution as a tuple of (real part, imaginary part), so both real and complex answers fit the same shape.
